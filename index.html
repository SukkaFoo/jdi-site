Outsourced Accounting

Your full finance function, run by people who've sat at the top of one.

Most outsourced accounting firms give you a process and a portal. We give you a Chartered Accountant who personally oversees your books — and has spent over two decades knowing exactly what a CFO needs to see, and when.

For US-based businesses in particular, this is where we see the biggest gap in the market. You're dealing with offshore accounting teams where the most senior person you'll ever speak to is a project manager. At JDI, the person reviewing your management accounts is the same person who has led multi-country finance organisations with revenues up to $1.7 billion, working across US GAAP and IFRS simultaneously. That context changes everything — from how your chart of accounts is structured to how your monthly pack is presented to your board.

We handle everything from first-entry bookkeeping through to audit-ready financial statements. But unlike a traditional accounting firm, every layer of the work is designed with the end output in mind — because we know what a CFO actually reads on a Monday morning.

03
Business Intelligence

Built by someone who has lived on both sides of the data.

Most BI implementations fail not because of the technology, but because the developers don't understand the business. Digby Beyleveld has spent his career closing that gap — as both a senior finance executive who depended on data to make decisions, and as the person who built the systems that produced it.

With deep expertise in Microsoft Fabric, Power BI, SQL, Azure, and AWS data platforms, Digby leads JDI's BI practice from technical strategy through to delivery. He has led large-scale ERP implementations and data engineering projects across multinational organisations — which means he understands not just how to build a dashboard, but how to design the data architecture underneath it so it doesn't break when the business scales or changes.

Our BI work is built in lockstep with your financial reporting. The numbers your dashboards show are consistent with your management accounts, statutory reports, and board pack. That coherence is rare — and it matters enormously when you're making decisions or presenting to investors.

"Good BI isn't about the dashboard. It's about the architecture underneath it — and knowing what questions the business will be asking in 18 months, not just today."

— Digby Beyleveld, Finance & Business Intelligence Executive
